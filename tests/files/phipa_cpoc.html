What we do

The mission of the Citizens Police Oversight Commission (CPOC) is to serve the community by helping to improve the relationship between the police and the community.

Through our work we:

  • Analyze the policies, practices, and customs of the Philadelphia Police Department.
  • Review police department policies and procedures.
  • Hold public meetings to discuss issues around police matters.

We provide recommendations on how to improve the police department with the mayor, city managing director, and police commissioner.

CPOC is a member of the National Association for Civilian Oversight of Law Enforcement (NACOLE). For more information about police oversight around the U.S., visit NACOLE.

    CPOC March Virtual Town Hall

    March 21, 2023
    6:00 pm to 7:30 pm, 2 hours

    Please join the Citizens Police Oversight Commission on Tuesday, March 21st from 6:00 PM - 7:30 PM for our March Community Town Hall. After the death of Walter Wallace, Jr. in October 2020, many in the community wanted PPD to implement changes to how mental health and crisis calls were handled by Police.

    This month we’ll be joined by members of the PPD Behavioral Health Unit, which was created to provide health-centered harm reduction focused responses to vulnerable populations that routinely interact with police. The BHU will talk to the community about how they function and what the community can expect when they respond to mental health/crisis calls.  We will also discuss Police Assisted Diversion, a program that diverts individuals arrested for low-level offenses away from jail and towards treatment and resources. If you’re curious about some of the changes PPD is making regarding mental health support, you won’t want to miss this meeting!

    CPOC Community Movie Night & Discussion

    March 31, 2023
    5:30 pm to 8:30 pm, 3 hours
    Parkway Central Library, 1901 Vine St, Philadelphia, PA 19103, USA
    map
    On Friday, March 31st at 5:30 PM, CPOC will be hosting a movie night at the Parkway Central Library. We will be watching The Picture Taker, a film about Earnest Withers, a famed civil rights photographer who left a complicated legacy after it was revealed after his death that he was actually an FBI informant. After watching the documentary, we will hold a group discussion on police surveillance and its effects on the Black community during the civil rights era. For more information contact CPOC at 215-685-0891 or CPOC@phila.gov.
